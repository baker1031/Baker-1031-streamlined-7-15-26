Who We Are
A specialist desk, not a storefront.
Most exchanges are won or lost on two things: the quality of the replacement options on the table, and the discipline to move before the 45- and 180-day clocks run out. We exist to give accredited investors both.
We are independent and sponsor-agnostic. We do not manufacture our own deals or push proprietary product. Our work is to understand an investor's exchange, surface the offerings that actually fit, and bring institutional-grade diligence to a decision that has to be made on a deadline.
We serve accredited investors only, because the strategies we work in — DSTs and private real-estate securities — are sold exclusively to them.

The Heritage
Three generations on the same side of the table.
The name carries a history. Real estate has been the family's business across three generations — through cycles, downturns, and recoveries that taught a simple lesson: the deal matters, but the standard you hold it to matters more.
That inheritance shows up in how we work. We have sat on the ownership side of buildings, not just the brokerage side of trades, and we bring that operator's instinct to every sponsor and offering we evaluate. It is also why accountability is not a marketing word here — it is the family business.

Common Questions
About Baker 1031
What is Baker 1031 Investments?
Baker 1031 Investments is an independent real-estate-securities brokerage in San Francisco that helps accredited investors complete 1031 exchanges using Delaware Statutory Trusts (DSTs), and also works in Qualified Opportunity Zone funds, 721 UPREIT exchanges, mineral and royalty interests, and REITs. Securities are offered through Aurora Securities, Inc., member FINRA/SIPC.
Is Baker 1031 independent?
Yes. Baker 1031 is independent and sponsor-agnostic — not owned by, and not exclusively representing, any single sponsor. We do not manufacture our own deals or push proprietary product; our coverage spans the institutional DST and 1031 universe, and our job is to find the right fit for the investor.
Who can work with Baker 1031?
We serve accredited investors only, because the strategies we work in — DSTs and private real-estate securities — are sold exclusively to them under SEC Rule 501 of Regulation D.
Who is the broker-dealer, and how is Baker 1031 paid?
Securities are offered through Aurora Securities, Inc. — member FINRA/SIPC. Compensation comes from selling commissions paid by the sponsor as part of each offering's disclosed load, not as an added fee to the investor; every fee is disclosed in the offering's private placement memorandum.
