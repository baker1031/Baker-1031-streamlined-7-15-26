Background
From Wall Street real estate to the 1031 desk
Gerald F. Baker, III (&ldquo;Jerry&rdquo;) is the founder and managing principal of Baker 1031 Investments, LLC, specializing in institutional-grade Delaware Statutory Trust (DST) properties and tax-deferred exchange solutions for individual investors.
Prior to founding the firm, Jerry spent more than a decade on Wall Street, where he was directly involved in over $10 billion in real estate transactions, leading property investment strategies and acquisitions for several prominent private equity firms and hedge funds.
His expertise is underpinned by a 60-year family legacy in real estate — three generations of developing, managing, and investing in high-quality assets. He established Baker 1031 to address the lack of transparency in the 1031 marketplace, engineering an execution model that can complete complex transactions in as little as two to three days.
Before a single offering is shown, Jerry underwrites the underlying real estate the way a private-equity investor would — the market, the rent roll, the business plan, the debt — on the conviction that if the real estate does not make sense, the rest of the deal will not either.
At Baker 1031
What he does
Jerry serves as both the firm's broker and the architect of each client's replacement portfolio, building diversified allocations from institutional sponsors — names such as Blackstone, Hines, Apollo, Ares, ExchangeRight, and Cantor Fitzgerald — and coordinating the exchange against the 45- and 180-day clocks. He is also the author of the firm's Investing Memos.
The &ldquo;Better Call Jerry&rdquo; approach. Clients work directly with the founder — not a call center. Most DST allocations can close in two to three business days, and Jerry is reachable on a direct line throughout the exchange.
