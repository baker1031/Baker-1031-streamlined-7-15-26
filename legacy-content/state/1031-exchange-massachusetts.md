Massachusetts FAQ
What is the capital gains tax rate in Massachusetts? Massachusetts taxes capital gains as ordinary income, up to 9%, with no separate long-term rate. Combined with the federal 20% rate and the 3.8% net investment income tax, a high-bracket Massachusetts seller can face roughly ~32.8% on a real estate gain. Massachusetts applies a 4% 'millionaire' surtax above ~$1M, lifting the top rate to 9%. Does Massachusetts recognize 1031 exchanges? Yes. Massachusetts conforms to IRC §1031, so a properly structured exchange defers Massachusetts tax as well as federal tax. Why use a 1031 exchange in Massachusetts? To defer the tax on a large gain (up to about ~32.8% combined) and move from active landlording into passive, professionally managed real estate while keeping your full equity invested.
