Mississippi taxes capital gains as ordinary income — up to 4% — stacked on top of federal tax, so selling appreciated real estate can cost roughly ~27.8% of the gain. A 1031 exchange into a Delaware Statutory Trust lets Mississippi investors defer that combined bill and trade active landlording for passive institutional real estate.

Mississippi FAQ
What is the capital gains tax rate in Mississippi? Mississippi taxes capital gains as ordinary income, up to 4%, with no separate long-term rate. Combined with the federal 20% rate and the 3.8% net investment income tax, a high-bracket Mississippi seller can face roughly ~27.8% on a real estate gain. Does Mississippi recognize 1031 exchanges? Yes. Mississippi conforms to IRC §1031, so a properly structured exchange defers Mississippi tax as well as federal tax. Why use a 1031 exchange in Mississippi? To defer the tax on a large gain (up to about ~27.8% combined) and move from active landlording into passive, professionally managed real estate while keeping your full equity invested. These are Regulation D offerings for accredited investors.
