Maine FAQ
What is the capital gains tax rate in Maine? Maine taxes capital gains as ordinary income, up to 7.15%, with no separate long-term rate. Combined with the federal 20% rate and the 3.8% net investment income tax, a high-bracket Maine seller can face roughly ~31.0% on a real estate gain. Does Maine recognize 1031 exchanges? Yes. Maine conforms to IRC §1031, so a properly structured exchange defers Maine tax as well as federal tax. Why use a 1031 exchange in Maine? To defer the tax on a large gain (up to about ~31.0% combined) and move from active landlording into passive, professionally managed real estate while keeping your full equity invested.
