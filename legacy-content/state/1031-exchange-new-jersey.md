New Jersey FAQ
What is the capital gains tax rate in New Jersey? New Jersey taxes capital gains as ordinary income, up to 10.75%, with no separate long-term rate. Combined with the federal 20% rate and the 3.8% net investment income tax, a high-bracket New Jersey seller can face roughly ~34.5% on a real estate gain. Does New Jersey recognize 1031 exchanges? Yes. New Jersey conforms to IRC §1031, so a properly structured exchange defers New Jersey tax as well as federal tax. Why use a 1031 exchange in New Jersey? To defer the tax on a large gain (up to about ~34.5% combined) and move from active landlording into passive, professionally managed real estate while keeping your full equity invested.
