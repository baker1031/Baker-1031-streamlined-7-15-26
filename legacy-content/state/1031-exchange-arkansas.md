Arkansas FAQ
What is the capital gains tax rate in Arkansas? Arkansas taxes capital gains as ordinary income, up to 3.9%, with no separate long-term rate. Combined with the federal 20% rate and the 3.8% net investment income tax, a high-bracket Arkansas seller can face roughly ~27.7% on a real estate gain. Arkansas excludes 50% of net long-term capital gains, which lowers the effective rate below the 3.9% ordinary top rate. Does Arkansas recognize 1031 exchanges? Yes. Arkansas conforms to IRC §1031, so a properly structured exchange defers Arkansas tax as well as federal tax. Why use a 1031 exchange in Arkansas? To defer the tax on a large gain (up to about ~27.7% combined) and move from active landlording into passive, professionally managed real estate while keeping your full equity invested.
